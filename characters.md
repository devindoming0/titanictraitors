# TITANIC TRAITORS — Character Guide

Eight iconic figures from the voyage. Each player embodies one character for the duration of the cruise.

---

## 1. JACK DAWSON
*The Charming Drifter*

> "I figure life's a gift and I don't intend on wasting it."

A penniless artist who won his third-class ticket in a poker game. Jack is quick-witted, disarmingly warm, and sees the best in people — which makes him dangerously naïve to betrayal.

**How to play Jack:** Be open, enthusiastic, and make friends easily. Everyone trusts Jack. Use that goodwill.

**Optional Ability — Sketch Artist:**
Once per game, Jack may secretly observe one player for an entire day and "sketch" them. That evening at dinner, before nominations, Jack privately tells the host which player he observed. The host privately tells Jack whether that player is Faithful or Traitor. Jack may not reveal the host's answer directly — only hint at what he "saw in their eyes."

---

## 2. ROSE DeWITT BUKATER
*The Rebel Heir*

> "I saw my whole life as if I'd already lived it — an endless parade of parties and cotillions, yachts, and polo matches."

First-class and fiercely independent, Rose is done playing the role assigned to her. She is perceptive, bold, and unafraid to name what others won't. She will be heard.

**How to play Rose:** Be the first to speak your mind. Dominate dinner conversation. Be dramatically certain — even when you're guessing.

**Optional Ability — Heart of the Ocean:**
Once per game, Rose may veto a banishment. After the vote is counted but before the result is announced, Rose may stand up and declare *"I will not allow this."* The vote is immediately re-held with Rose's nominee removed from the ballot. Rose may only use this if she genuinely believes the wrong person is being banished. This ability cannot be used on Day 6.

---

## 3. CAL HOCKLEY
*The Steel Heir*

> "I make my own luck."

A man of enormous wealth and boundless arrogance. Cal expects to get what he wants and has never been told no by anyone who mattered. He is calculating, manipulative, and utterly convinced of his own superiority.

**How to play Cal:** Command the room. Look slightly inconvenienced by accusations. Make others feel small for even suggesting it.

**Optional Ability — Bribe:**
Once per game, Cal may secretly offer another player a deal: *"Vote how I tell you tonight, and I'll protect you from nomination tomorrow."* The other player may accept or refuse. If they accept and keep the deal, Cal owes them one nomination immunity the next day. Cal has no obligation to honor the deal if he's the Traitor.

---

## 4. RUTH DeWITT BUKATER
*The Iron Matriarch*

> "I will not have it. This is not a discussion."

Cold, tactical, and obsessed with appearances, Ruth sees every dinner as a chess match. She knows exactly who is useful and who is expendable. She will not be caught off guard.

**How to play Ruth:** Be composed at all times. Never show emotion about a murder. Redirect every accusation with a cutting observation about someone else.

**Optional Ability — Social Register:**
Once per game, Ruth may formally second a nomination before it is spoken aloud — meaning she announces that she will be supporting a specific player's banishment before the nominating player can be named. This forces that player's name into the nomination pool regardless of whether anyone else nominates them. It signals certainty and rattles the table.

---

## 5. MOLLY BROWN
*The Unsinkable One*

> "I'm not going to let a little cold water stop me."

A self-made woman from Colorado who has earned every inch of her first-class cabin through sheer force of personality. Molly is loud, warm, generous, and impossible to ignore.

**How to play Molly:** Be the life of every dinner. Make people laugh. Defend underdogs loudly. And when you've had enough, call it like it is.

**Optional Ability — The Lifeboat:**
Once per game, Molly may protect one living player from being murdered that night. After the banishment dinner, she must privately tell the host who she is protecting before midnight. If the Traitor chose that player to murder, the murder fails — and Molly announces the next morning that she *"had a feeling"* and stayed up all night with that person. The protected player is known publicly. The Traitor is not.

---

## 6. THOMAS ANDREWS
*The Architect*

> "She's the most beautiful thing I've ever seen. But... I know what she can't do."

The man who built the Titanic knows its every weakness. Andrews is gentle, brilliant, and burdened by knowledge. He sees structural flaws in people the same way he saw them in ships — quietly, and too late to warn anyone.

**How to play Thomas:** Be soft-spoken and precise. When you speak, people listen. You don't accuse often — but when you do, it lands.

**Optional Ability — Blueprints:**
Once per game, Andrews may "review the ship's manifest" — he privately tells the host that he is using his ability, and the host tells him the role (Traitor or Faithful) of one randomly selected living player (not Andrews himself). Andrews may share this information however he wishes — or keep it to himself.

---

## 7. CAPTAIN EDWARD SMITH
*The Commander*

> "I've been Captain of this vessel for thirty years. I know what I'm doing."

The most respected man aboard. Smith carries authority effortlessly — when he speaks, the room quiets. He is measured, proud, and deeply reluctant to admit error.

**How to play Captain Smith:** Speak deliberately and rarely. Defer to evidence. Never show panic. When you commit to an accusation, it should feel like a verdict.

**Optional Ability — Ship's Order:**
Once per game, Captain Smith may call for a **re-vote** immediately after a vote result is announced. He stands, announces *"We will vote again,"* and the vote is repeated with the same nominees. The second result is final. This power cannot be used on Day 6 or if the initial vote was unanimous.

---

## 8. CELINE DION
*The Songstress*

> "Near, far, wherever you are... I believe that the heart does go on."

The legendary Celine Dion is performing aboard the Titanic on a very special engagement. She is radiant, emotional, deeply intuitive, and her music has a way of making people confess things they shouldn't.

**How to play Celine:** Be theatrical. React to every murder announcement with performative grief. Break into brief song when the mood requires it. And pay very close attention to who doesn't react at all.

**Optional Ability — My Heart Will Go On:**
Once per game, Celine may invoke the spirit of a murdered player. She announces this at morning gathering on any day after the first murder. She may ask one yes/no question to the ghost of a previously murdered player — that eliminated player answers honestly (including Traitors, who must answer truthfully as their ghost). The question must be asked aloud in front of the group.

---

## CHARACTER ASSIGNMENT GUIDE

For groups smaller than 8, remove characters in this suggested order (remove from the bottom of the list first):

| Players | Remove |
|---------|--------|
| 7       | Captain Edward Smith |
| 6       | Captain Edward Smith + Ruth DeWitt Bukater |
| 5       | Above + Cal Hockley |
| 4       | Above + Thomas Andrews |
| 3       | Above + Molly Brown |

This keeps Jack, Rose, and Celine in every game — the emotional core of the voyage.

---

*Abilities are optional. Use them for a richer experience with players who enjoy hidden information. Skip them for a faster, purer social deduction game.*
