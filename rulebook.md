# TITANIC TRAITORS
### A Social Deduction Game for 3–8 Players · Designed for a 6-Day Cruise

---

## OVERVIEW

It is April 10, 1912. You and your companions have boarded the RMS Titanic for the voyage of a lifetime. But not everyone aboard has good intentions.

Hidden among the faithful passengers is a **Traitor** — a cold-blooded murderer who intends to eliminate every last one of them before the ship reaches New York. The faithful must identify and **banish** the Traitor before it's too late. The Traitor must **murder** the faithful one by one, in secret, until none remain to oppose them.

Can you survive all six days at sea?

## SETUP

### Before the Cruise Departs

1. **Assign Characters.** Randomly deal one Character Card to each player. Players should keep their character secret until the game begins, or reveal it publicly — your group decides.

2. **Assign Roles.** Shuffle the correct number of Role Cards (see table above) face-down. Deal one to each player privately. Each player reads their role in secret and keeps it hidden.

3. **Traitors Meet.** On the first night of the cruise (Night 0, before Day 1), all Traitors must secretly identify themselves to one another. Do this by:
   - Having everyone close their eyes.
   - The host says: *"Traitors, open your eyes and look around."*
   - Traitors make eye contact and silently acknowledge each other.
   - *"Everyone close your eyes. Everyone open your eyes."*

4. **Seal Your Role.** Optionally, players seal their Role Card in an envelope and carry it with them throughout the cruise. It is only opened upon elimination or at game end.

## THE SIX DAYS

The game is structured around the six days of the cruise. Each day follows this sequence:

### Morning — The Body Is Found

> Skip this on Day 1 (no murder has occurred yet).

One hour after the previous night's banishment dinner, the host privately tells all players who was murdered. (Or sends a group message, or leaves a note under their cabin door — your group decides.) The murdered player is **eliminated**: they may no longer vote and cannot reveal their role until the game ends.

In the morning, players gather and openly discuss who was murdered, what it means, and who might be responsible.

### Daytime — Social Deduction

Players spend the day in their natural cruise activities. This is the heart of the game. Faithful players should:

- Discuss suspicions openly or in private
- Observe behavior carefully
- Form alliances and share theories

Traitors should:
- Deflect suspicion
- Subtly mislead
- Decide who to murder that night

**Optional Rule — Suspicious Behavior:**
If using this rule (see Optional Rules), each Traitor must perform their assigned suspicious behavior at some point during the day. Faithful players may call out a behavior by name at the Banishment Dinner to use it as evidence.

### Evening Dinner — The Banishment Vote

Every evening, all **living players** gather for dinner. This is the formal accusation and voting phase.

**1. Open Accusations**
Any player may accuse another of being the Traitor. Accusations should be argued with evidence, reasoning, or gut feeling. There is no limit to how many players can be accused.

**2. Final Nominees**
Players nominate up to 3 players for banishment. Each nomination requires a second (another player must agree). The 3 players with the most nominations go to the final vote. In case of a tie for the third spot, all tied players are included.

**3. The Vote**
All living players (including nominees) simultaneously vote for one of the final nominees by pointing at them on the count of three. The player with the most votes is **banished** and eliminated from the game.
- In case of a tie, the player who has been most recently accused (or chosen by the host as tiebreaker) is banished.
- Banished players must immediately leave the table without revealing their role.

**4. The Reveal**
The banished player opens their sealed envelope (or simply announces) whether they were a **Traitor** or a **Faithful**. Their Character Card is also revealed.

Check for **win conditions** after each banishment (see Win Conditions below).

### Night — The Murder

After the dinner ends and banished players have left, the night phase begins.

> The Traitor(s) must submit their murder target within one hour of the banishment dinner ending. This is done privately — by text to the host, by a note passed to the host, or by whatever secret method your group decides.

- The Traitor chooses **one living Faithful** to murder.
- If there are two Traitors, they must agree on a single target. They may coordinate secretly (in person, by text, etc.) within the one-hour window.
- The Traitor **cannot murder a Traitor** (themselves or their partner).
- The host records the murder but does not announce it until the **next morning** (one hour after the next dinner, or at the start of the next day — your group decides).

---

## WIN CONDITIONS

Check after every banishment and every murder:

**Faithful Win** — All Traitors have been banished. The game ends immediately. Surviving Faithfuls celebrate. All roles are revealed.

**Traitor Win** — The number of living Traitors equals or exceeds the number of living Faithfuls. The Traitor(s) reveal themselves and claim victory.

**Stalemate (Day 6 End)** — If the cruise ends and neither side has won, a Final Vote is held at disembarkation dinner with all living players. No murder follows. If the Traitor is banished, Faithfuls win. If not, Traitors win.

## OPTIONAL RULES

### Suspicious Behavior

Before the game begins, the host secretly assigns each Traitor one **Suspicious Behavior** from the list below (or invents their own). The Traitor must visibly perform this behavior at least once per day they are still in the game. Faithful players may notice and cite it as evidence.

Suggested behaviors:
- Must stir their drink counterclockwise before every sip
- Must always sit with their back to a wall
- Must hum a few bars of *My Heart Will Go On* whenever they enter a room
- Cannot say the word "ship" — must say "vessel" instead
- Must always fold their napkin into a triangle at the table
- Must make eye contact with someone for exactly 3 seconds before speaking to them
- Must always reference the weather in conversation at least once per hour
- Must avoid saying any character's first name — only last names allowed

If a Traitor forgets to perform their behavior on a given day, the host may declare a **Strike**. Three strikes eliminate the optional rule for that Traitor (too risky for game balance to penalize with elimination).

### Character Abilities

See `characters.md` for each character's optional special ability. These are activated once per game and add a hidden-information layer to the game. Agree before sailing whether abilities are in play.

### Ghost Whisper

When a Faithful is murdered (not banished), they become a **Ghost**. A Ghost may whisper one piece of information to one living player of their choice before midnight on the day their death is announced. The Ghost must physically whisper (or privately message) a single sentence. They cannot name the Traitor directly — only share what they observed.

### Locked Room

Once per game, any living player may invoke a **Locked Room** by announcing it at dinner before nominations. For that night only, the invoking player is immune from murder. This can only be used once per player per game, and only one player may invoke it per night (first to declare wins).

## HOST RESPONSIBILITIES

One person (ideally not playing) should serve as **Host** for the cruise. The Host:

- Distributes and collects role assignments
- Receives murder submissions privately each night
- Announces murders each morning
- Facilitates the evening dinner vote
- Adjudicates disputes
- Tracks all eliminations

If no dedicated host is available, assign the role to a player — they still play normally, but handle logistics. In this case, murder submissions must go to a neutral third party (e.g., a group chat where only the host can see messages, or physical notes).

## EXAMPLE ROUND

**Setup:** 6 players. Characters: Jack, Rose, Cal, Molly, Thomas, Celine. Roles: 1 Traitor (Cal), 5 Faithful.

**Night 0:** All close eyes. Cal opens his eyes and looks around — he's alone. He knows he's the sole Traitor.

**Day 1:** Players meet on deck. Cal deflects, accuses Jack half-heartedly, charms the table.

**Day 1 Evening Dinner:** Rose accuses Cal. Cal turns it back on Thomas. Nominations: Cal, Thomas, Molly. Vote goes 3-2-1 to Thomas. Thomas is banished. He opens his envelope: **Faithful**. Groans around the table.

**Night 1:** Cal submits his murder target — Molly.

**Day 2 Morning:** Players learn Molly was murdered. 4 players remain: Jack, Rose, Cal, Celine.

**Day 2 Evening Dinner:** Suspicion mounts on Cal. He has 2 votes, Rose has 1, Jack has 1. Cal is banished. He opens his envelope: **Traitor**. Faithfuls win.

## TIPS FOR TRAITORS

- Don't be the first to accuse. Let others set the tone.
- Accuse someone you like — it signals trust.
- Vote with the crowd. Outlier votes make you suspicious.
- Kill someone unexpected. Murdering the most vocal accuser is a tell.
- If there are two Traitors, don't make eye contact with your partner too much during dinner.

## TIPS FOR FAITHFULS

- Track who votes for whom each evening.
- The Traitor rarely volunteers to be a nominee.
- Notice who reacts oddly when a murder is announced.
- Trust your gut — but corroborate before committing.
- In small groups (3–4), one wrong banishment can end the game.

---

## GAME END CEREMONY

When the game ends (win or lose), all players reveal their Role Cards simultaneously. The group discusses:

- Did the Traitor(s) behave suspiciously?
- What was the pivotal vote?
- Who would have been next?

The Traitor(s) may now reveal everything they hid. No hard feelings — it's all part of the game.
